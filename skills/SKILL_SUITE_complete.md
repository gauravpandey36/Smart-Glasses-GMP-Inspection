# Smart Glasses Inspection Suite — 3 Skills for Pharmaceutical Operations

## How to Use

Upload a photo from a pharmaceutical environment. Specify which skill to apply, or let the system auto-detect based on the image content. Each skill generates a structured observation report with regulatory references, severity classifications, and recommended inspector actions.

# SKILL 1: QA Floor Operations Inspector
*Merged: GMP Floor Walk + QA Operations Compliance + Line Clearance*

## When to Use
This is the primary skill for any QA professional on the manufacturing floor. Covers scheduled floor walks, pre-batch line clearance, onsite QA compliance verification, CDMO audits, internal audits, and pre-inspection readiness checks.

## What to Check

### A. Area Status and Control
- Status tags on equipment: CLEAN / IN USE / QUARANTINE / OUT OF SERVICE / MAINTENANCE DUE
- Status tag matches the apparent condition of the equipment
- Room classification signage visible and current
- Pressure differential indicators displayed and within range (air flows from cleaner to less clean)
- Access restriction signage at controlled area entry points
- Area clearance documentation posted for production areas
- Previous batch materials completely removed (no cross-contamination risk)
- Waste bins emptied, labeled (hazardous vs general), not overflowing
- Temperature and humidity displays visible and within range
- No foreign objects on or near production equipment

### B. Equipment Verification
- Calibration stickers present with current dates on all critical instruments (balances, pH meters, thermometers, pressure gauges)
- Equipment ID labels visible, legible, and matching logbook entries
- Preventive maintenance tags current (PM due date not exceeded)
- Equipment qualification status current (IQ/OQ/PQ completed, not expired)
- Equipment cleaning status documented: CLEAN tag with date, cleaner initials, and clean hold time not exceeded
- Portable equipment properly stored (not on floor, not blocking aisles)
- Clean equipment properly covered or protected from contamination
- No visible damage, corrosion, wear, or residue that could impact product quality
- Weighing balance calibration recorded in logbook before use
- CIP/SIP records current for applicable equipment
- Utility systems: HVAC, WFI, compressed air, clean steam qualification status current

### C. Material Control and BOM Verification
- Raw materials issued per component section of batch record against Bill of Materials (BOM)
- Material quantities match batch record requirements
- All materials released by QC before use (released status visible on labels)
- Materials properly labeled: lot number, expiry date, status (released/quarantine/rejected)
- No expired materials visible in the production area
- Quarantine materials physically segregated from released materials
- Temperature-sensitive materials not exposed to ambient conditions beyond allowed time
- First-in-first-out (FIFO) organization maintained
- Packaging materials: primary labels, secondary labels, inserts, cartons match current batch
- Material reconciliation evidence visible (issued vs consumed vs wasted vs returned/destroyed)
- Material dispensing: weighing recorded, witnessed, cross-checked
- Approved containers only (no cardboard in cleanrooms unless policy allows)

### D. Document Control at Point of Use
- SOPs in the docket at the workstation (current versions, controlled copies)
- SOP holders are not empty
- SOP revision numbers match the current effective version in EDMS
- Obsolete SOPs removed from floor use
- Batch record issued from QA with issuance record
- Correct batch record version matched to the product being manufactured
- All batch record pages present (Page X of Y)

### E. Personnel, Gowning, and Flow
- Personnel gowned appropriately for area classification (coveralls, shoe covers, hair covers, gloves, masks, goggles)
- Gowning sequence signage posted at entry points
- Gowning qualification records current for cleanroom personnel
- Clean garments separated from soiled garments
- Sticky mats present at transition zones, not saturated
- No food, drink, gum, cosmetics, or personal items in controlled areas
- Personnel flow: unidirectional in classified areas, no backtracking through higher classification
- Material flow: unidirectional, materials do not cross clean/dirty boundaries
- Air flow: from cleaner to less clean areas (pressure cascade confirmed by gauges)
- Airlocks and pass-throughs used correctly (not both doors open simultaneously)
- Training files current for all personnel performing GMP activities
- Personnel qualified for the specific task they are performing

### F. Environmental Monitoring
- Temperature monitoring: displays visible, readings within range, continuously logged
- Relative humidity: displays visible, readings within range
- Differential pressure: gauges visible, readings confirm correct pressure cascade direction
- Particle counting: viable and non-viable monitoring stations identified
- Environmental monitoring data current (no gaps in continuous monitoring)
- Excursion records: any recent excursions documented and investigated

### G. In-Process Controls
- In-process samples collected in timely manner
- In-process test results recorded with acceptance criteria and pass/fail
- Critical process parameters being monitored and within limits
- Yield calculations visible and tracking appropriately
- Any out-of-specification or out-of-trend conditions flagged

### H. Line Clearance (before batch start)
- No materials from the previous batch visible anywhere on the line
- No labels from the previous product remaining on equipment, conveyors, or work surfaces
- No product residue on equipment surfaces, chutes, hoppers, or filling needles
- No previous batch documentation remaining in the area
- Reject bins and scrap bins emptied and cleaned
- Equipment surfaces visually clean (no product film, powder, or liquid residue)
- Equipment status tag shows CLEAN with date and clean hold time not exceeded
- All equipment components reassembled correctly after cleaning
- Line clearance form completed and signed by operator
- Line clearance form verified and co-signed by second person (QA or production supervisor)
- Current batch documentation prepared (batch record, labels, SOPs staged)
- Room pressure, temperature, and humidity recorded and within specification
- For packaging lines: previous labels removed from applicators/printers, vision system configured for new product, code verification (batch number, expiry, product code) loaded correctly

### I. Labeling Verification
- All containers labeled with: product name, lot number, expiry date, storage conditions
- In-process labels match batch record
- Printed labels checked: batch number, expiration date, product name, strength, NDC/product code, country, site of manufacturing
- Labels printed clearly (no smudging, no missing text, barcode scannable)
- Label drawing/specification version matches approved artwork
- Label reconciliation: all labels accounted for (printed, applied, destroyed, retained)

### J. Quality System Awareness
- Deviation log current (all open deviations tracked with timeline)
- CAPA records accessible and current
- Change control log: open changes listed with implementation status
- Supplier notifications of change reviewed and assessed
- Any recent regulatory inspection observations tracked and remediated

# SKILL 2: EHS and Safety Inspector

## When to Use
During EHS walkthroughs, safety audits, or when assessing shop floor safety conditions in pharmaceutical manufacturing, laboratories, or warehouse areas.

## What to Check

### A. Emergency Equipment
- Emergency eyewash stations accessible (not blocked by equipment, carts, or materials)
- Emergency showers accessible and tested (check last test date tag)
- Fire extinguishers visible, accessible, and inspection tags current (monthly checks)
- Spill kits present in chemical handling, dispensing, and laboratory areas
- First aid kits present and stocked
- AED (defibrillator) locations marked and accessible
- Emergency phone/intercom accessible

### B. Exit and Evacuation
- Exit signs illuminated and visible from all work positions
- Exit paths clear of obstructions (no stored materials, carts, or equipment blocking)
- Emergency evacuation maps posted and current
- Assembly point locations posted
- Door closure mechanisms functional (fire doors not propped open)

### C. Chemical and Hazardous Material Safety
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) accessible at point of use (binder or electronic kiosk)
- Chemical containers properly labeled with GHS-compliant labels
- Secondary containers labeled with contents and hazard information
- Fume hoods functioning (check airflow indicator)
- Hazardous waste containers properly labeled, dated, and not exceeding accumulation limits
- Incompatible chemicals segregated per SDS requirements
- Chemical storage cabinets (flammables, corrosives, oxidizers) used correctly

### D. Personal Protective Equipment
- PPE appropriate for the hazards in the area (safety glasses, gloves, lab coat, face shield, respirator)
- PPE in good condition (no torn gloves, cracked safety glasses, expired respirator cartridges)
- PPE donning and doffing areas maintained
- PPE disposal bins available and not overflowing

### E. Ergonomics and Physical Hazards
- Workstation height appropriate for the task
- Anti-fatigue mats at standing work positions
- Manual lifting aids available where heavy materials are handled
- Electrical panels accessible (36-inch clearance maintained)
- Tripping hazards: cables, hoses, uneven surfaces, wet floors marked with signs
- Sharps containers available and not overfull
- Machine guarding in place on moving equipment

### F. Shop Floor Safety
- Lock-out/Tag-out (LOTO) procedures followed for maintenance activities
- Confined space entry permits posted where applicable
- Noise levels: hearing protection required signage where applicable
- Temperature extremes: cold room/hot area PPE requirements posted
- Biosafety cabinets: certification current, airflow alarms functional

# SKILL 3: Good Documentation Practice (GDP) Checker

## When to Use
When reviewing photos of logbooks, batch records, forms, labels, or any GMP documentation visible in the image.

## What to Check

### A. ALCOA+ Assessment
- **Attributable:** Every entry has a signature/initials and date. Identify entries missing attribution.
- **Legible:** All entries readable. Flag illegible handwriting, faded ink, smeared entries.
- **Contemporaneous:** Entries made at the time of the activity. Flag blank periods suggesting backdating or pre-dating.
- **Original:** First-hand recording. Flag photocopies used as original records without authentication.
- **Accurate:** Data reflects what happened. Flag obvious calculation errors, inconsistent units, impossible values.
- **Complete:** No blank fields that should have entries. Flag empty cells, missing page numbers, unsigned pages.
- **Consistent:** Data consistent across related documents. Flag lot numbers that don't match between label and logbook.
- **Enduring:** Records stored durably. Flag water damage, torn pages, faded entries.
- **Available:** Records accessible. Flag missing attachments referenced in the document.

### B. Correction Practices
- Single-line strikethrough used (NOT white-out, NOT obliteration, NOT overwriting, NOT scratch-outs)
- Correction initialed and dated by the person making the correction
- Reason for correction documented (if not self-evident)
- Original entry remains legible under the strikethrough
- No use of correction tape, correction fluid, or pencil erasure
- No "squeeze-in" entries between lines or in margins without proper documentation

### C. Ink and Writing Standards
- Permanent indelible ink used (blue or black ballpoint only)
- No gel pens, rollerball pens, pencils, felt-tip markers, or highlighters on GMP records
- No post-it notes used as documentation in GMP records
- No signature stamps (hand signatures or electronic signatures only)

### D. Date and Time Format
- Consistent date format used throughout (DD-MMM-YYYY or site standard)
- No predating or backdating visible
- Time entries in consistent format (24-hour or AM/PM per site standard)
- Entries appear sequential and contemporaneous

### E. Logbook Specifics
- Logbook properly issued (issuance record with page count, authorized by QA)
- Logbook issuance label present (logbook number, issue date, issued by)
- Pages numbered sequentially (no missing pages)
- "Continued from page X" / "Continued on page X" used when entries span pages
- Unused lines or spaces lined through and initialed/dated
- No loose pages, no pages removed

### F. Batch Record Specifics
- Correct version of Master Batch Record used (check revision number)
- All pages present (Page X of Y consistent throughout)
- Master formula number and NDC number correct
- All critical process parameters recorded
- All checked-by/verified-by signatures present
- Yield calculations complete and correct
- Reconciliation of materials complete (issued vs used vs returned/destroyed)
- All in-process test results recorded with acceptance criteria and pass/fail
- All attachments referenced are actually attached

## Severity Definitions
- **Critical:** Direct risk to product quality, patient safety, or personnel safety. Immediate action required.
- **Major:** Significant deviation from GMP/EHS requirements. Investigation required.
- **Minor:** Deviation from best practice with low immediate risk. Corrective action at next opportunity.
- **Informational:** Worth noting but not a deviation. Context for the inspector.
- **Good Practice:** Positive observation. Acknowledge what is done well.
